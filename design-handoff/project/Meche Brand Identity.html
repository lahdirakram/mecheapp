01 — Thesis

Why Mèche

In French, une mèche is a lock of hair — and a highlight. Être de mèche means to be in on the secret. The brand sits at exactly that intersection: salon-fluent, insider, knowing. The name is the product positioning.

02 — Logo system

06 — Voice

Insider, warm, knowing.

Write like a friend who knows a guy. Short sentences. French slips in when it earns its place. Never gatekeep, never lecture, never use the word luxe.

Do
On te trouve un coiffeur. Pas un salon.

Confident, plain, French-first. The product position in one line.

Don't
Discover premium hair experiences near you.

Generic app-speak. Could be any of fifteen apps. Says nothing.

Do
Booked. Léa sees you at 4. Don't be early — she hates that.

Specific, human, slightly cheeky. The product feels like a person texted you.

Don't
Your appointment has been successfully scheduled.

No salon ever talks like this. No friend ever talks like this.

Do
Être de mèche, c'est savoir qui coupe.

Plays on the idiom without explaining it. Trusts the audience.

Don't
The ultimate destination for luxury haircare ✨

Emoji sparkle, the word "luxury", "destination." All three are bans.

07 — Principles

Five rules.

When in doubt, return to these. They settle most design and copy arguments before they start.

01.

Insider, never gatekeeper.

The brand knows everyone. It introduces you. It doesn't make you prove anything.

02.

Book the cut, not the chair.

People follow hairdressers, not salons. The product, the photography, the copy — all of it puts the person first.

03.

French where it earns its place.

One French phrase per surface, maximum. Mèche, coupe, balayage, brushing. Words that already crossed the border.

04.

Beauty without performance.

No glitter, no rose-gold, no sparkle emoji. The brand is confident enough to be quiet.

05.

The grave is sacred.

The caramel accent over the e is the entire brand in one stroke. Never remove it, never recolor it outside the palette, never tilt it the wrong way. If the surface can't hold it, use the tile.
